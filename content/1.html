I'm quitting my job. I work as a backend software engineer at a small-ish tech company. I make around 100k a year, have excellent benefits (going to Disneyland once a year), and love my coworkers. There is no problem with any aspect of my employment. I love my job. It's consistent, manageable, and in my head, it's a respectable career. It's such a good feeling telling someone that I'm a programmer working for a tech company and seeing their reaction. It's not for show, but most people are impressed.

  So why quit? Many things have changed throughout the 26 years of my life, but one thing has stayed consistent. I've always wanted to be rich. When I was younger, it was: 15 fast cars in my garage, a huge movie room, and a bowling alley (I don't even like bowling that much). Now those things aren't as enticing. I've realized now that the thing that is more valuable than all is freedomâ€”choosing where I work and when I work. Deciding what I work on and who I work with. I don't want to be told what a company's values are. I want to live them. So yes, I still want to be rich, but now I have more profound, pure, underlying motivations to help me get there.

  I've been working as a software engineer for around four years now. I've learned so much. I'm much more skilled and understand the industry like a pro. That's about it, though. From that growth and knowledge, I haven't had anything to show for it that's mine. It's all been someone else's. Don't get me wrong, I'm grateful that my company took a chance on me (I dropped out of college with little coding experience), but something still needs to change. I need to quit my job if I'm going to one day live the life that I dreamed of when I was 12 years old.
